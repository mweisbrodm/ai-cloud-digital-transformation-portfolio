# GenAI Prompt Engineering for Customer Review Analysis

## Project Type
Generative AI | Prompt Engineering | Customer Experience | Business Analysis

## Executive Summary

This project explored how generative AI and structured prompt engineering can help organizations analyze customer reviews more efficiently. The goal was to classify feedback, identify themes, assign priorities, and recommend responses that support better customer experience management.

Rather than treating AI as a novelty, the project focused on a practical business use case: helping product managers and analysts respond to customer feedback faster and more consistently.

## Business Problem

Organizations receive large amounts of customer feedback through reviews, surveys, and service channels. Without a structured process, important issues can be missed, response times can vary, and teams may struggle to prioritize limited resources.

The project examined how AI-powered review categorization could help organizations identify urgent issues and turn unstructured feedback into actionable insight.

## Approach

1. Compared zero-shot, one-shot, and structured reasoning prompts.
2. Used role-based instructions to shape output.
3. Designed output formats for sentiment, tags, priority, suggested action, and customer response.
4. Evaluated prompt clarity, consistency, and usefulness.
5. Connected AI-generated outputs to a practical customer experience workflow.

## Key Business Questions

- How can AI help organizations process customer feedback more efficiently?
- How can prompts be structured to produce consistent and useful outputs?
- What information should be extracted from reviews to support business decisions?
- How can teams use AI without losing human judgment?

## Strategic Recommendations

- Create standardized prompt templates for recurring feedback analysis.
- Use AI to classify reviews by sentiment, topic, urgency, and suggested action.
- Keep human review in place for high-priority or sensitive customer issues.
- Test and refine prompts before operational use.
- Use AI outputs as decision support rather than automatic final decisions.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Designing prompts for practical business use
- Translating unstructured customer feedback into structured insight
- Evaluating AI output quality
- Thinking through responsible AI use
- Connecting AI tools to customer experience and operations

## Executive Takeaway

Generative AI can help organizations listen to customers at scale, but the value depends on how clearly the problem is defined and how thoughtfully the workflow is designed. Strong prompts are not just technical instructions; they are business process tools.
